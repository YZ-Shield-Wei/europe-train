London to Paris Eurostar Guide

2026 Latest: Eurostar Experience, Fare System, Immigration Process, Seat Selection

Mis à jour June 2026Reading time: 10 minutes

Key Highlights

  • London to Paris in just 2 hours 15 minutes, crossing the Channel Tunnel
  • Early bird tickets from €50, Standard Premier includes meals
  • Arrive 60 minutes before departure (30 minutes for Business)
  • Immigration done at the station, no airport queues
  • St Pancras and Gare du Nord are both city center stations

3. Immigration Process

Security Check Process

Step 1: Arrive at station (60 minutes early)
Step 2: Self check-in (print ticket or scan mobile)
Step 3: Security screening (airport-style but more relaxed)
Step 4: UK exit border control
Step 5: French entry border control
Step 6: Wait in departure lounge

4. Booking Tips

Money-Saving Checklist

  • Book 3–4 months in advance (early bird from €50)
  • Travel Tuesday to Thursday
  • Avoid holidays and summer peak season
  • Register for Eurostar membership (points accumulation)
  • Consider Standard Premier (includes meal, great value)

5. Station Guide

London St Pancras International

  • Tube: King's Cross St Pancras (Circle, Hammersmith, Metropolitan, Northern, Piccadilly, Victoria lines)
  • Highlights: Victorian Gothic architecture, Eurostar-dedicated platforms
  • Facilities: Free WiFi, multiple restaurants, Waitrose supermarket

Paris Gare du Nord

  • Metro: Gare du Nord (Lines 4, 5)
  • RER: Lines B, D, E
  • Highlights: Europe's busiest station, piano performances
  • Facilities: Luggage storage, multiple restaurants, Monoprix supermarket
