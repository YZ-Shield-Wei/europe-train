欧洲铁路通票指南

2026 完整指南: Eurail, Interrail, Swiss Travel Pass, and country-specific passes

更新 June 2026Reading time: 15 minutes

Key Takeaways

  • Eurail Pass for non-European residents, Interrail for European residents
  • Choose Global Pass for multi-country trips, One Country Pass for single-country
  • Swiss Travel Pass is the best value for Switzerland
  • Calculate break-even point: pass price vs individual tickets
  • Most passes require seat reservations (€5-20) for high-speed trains

2. Pass Types

Global Pass

  • Flexible: 4, 5, 7, 10, or 15 travel days within 1-2 months
  • Continuous: 15, 22 days, or 1-3 months unlimited
  • Best for: Multi-country trips (3+ countries)

One Country Pass

  • France: From €232 (3 days)
  • Germany: From €194 (3 days)
  • Italy: From €194 (3 days)
  • Switzerland: Not available (use Swiss Travel Pass)

3. Swiss Travel Pass

Why Swiss Travel Pass is Special

Includes:
- Unlimited train travel (including scenic trains)
- Free bus and tram travel
- Free boat cruises on lakes
- Free museum entry (500+ museums)
- 50% discount on mountain railways
- 25% discount on scenic trains (Glacier Express, Bernina Express)

Prices:
- 3 days: CHF 232
- 4 days: CHF 281
- 8 days: CHF 389
- 15 days: CHF 469

5. Seat Reservations

Reservation Checklist

  • TGV (France): €10-20, mandatory
  • ICE (Germany): €4.50, optional but recommended
  • Frecciarossa (Italy): €10, mandatory
  • AVE (Spain): €6.50, mandatory
  • Eurostar: €30, mandatory
  • European Star Red Line: €5-15, mandatory

Important Tips

Book reservations early (up to 3 months ahead) for popular routes. Summer and holidays fill up quickly. Some scenic trains require reservations months in advance (Glacier Express, Bernina Express).
