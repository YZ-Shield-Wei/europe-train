Berlin to Munich by Train

Complete 2026 Guide | From €17.90

The Berlin to Munich route is Germany's busiest high-speed rail corridor, covering 623km in just 4 hours. With Deutsche Bahn's ICE (Intercity-Express) service, this journey combines efficiency, comfort, and stunning views of the German countryside.

Quick Facts

  • Fastest journey: 3h 52m (ICE direct)
  • Average journey: 4h 15m
  • Direct trains: Yes, every hour
  • Main operator: Deutsche Bahn (ICE)
  • Price range: €17.90 - €150+
  • Best booking window: 2-3 months in advance

Route Overview

ICE Direct Service

Berlin Hauptbahnhof → München Hauptbahnhof

  • Distance: 623km
  • Travel time: 3h 52m - 4h 30m
  • Frequency: Every hour (peak times every 30 minutes)
  • Advantage: Fastest option, no changes required

Alternative Routes

Via Nuremberg (Scenic)

  • Berlin → Nuremberg → Munich
  • Travel time: ~5 hours
  • Advantage: Explore Nuremberg's medieval old town

Booking Tips & Money-Saving Strategies

1. Book Early for Sparpreis Fares

Deutsche Bahn's Sparpreis (saver fare) starts from €17.90 for advance bookings (2-3 months ahead). These tickets are valid on specific trains and cannot be refunded. Set price alerts on the DB Navigator app to catch the best deals.

2. Use BahnCard for Discounts

BahnCard 25 offers 25% off all fares (€62.90/year). BahnCard 50 gives 50% off flexible fares (€255/year). For frequent travelers, the BahnCard pays for itself quickly. Students and seniors get additional discounts.

3. Travel Off-Peak

Weekday trains between 9am and 2pm are typically cheaper than morning rush hour (7-9am) and Friday evening departures. Avoid holiday weekends for better availability.

4. Group Travel Savings

Groups of 6+ can use Gruppenreise (group travel) discounts. For 2-5 travelers, the Quer-durchs-Land-Ticket (€44 for up to 5 people) covers regional trains for a day.

  • Onboard bistro serving hot meals, snacks, and drinks

Service Classes

  • First Class: Wider seats, included meals, access to DB Lounges
  • Second Class: Comfortable seating, bistro access, same speed
  • ICE Sprinter: Premium service with fewer stops (3h 52m)

What to See Along the Way

Leipzig (Stopover Option)

Germany's fastest-growing city offers the Bach Museum, St. Thomas Church, and vibrant coffee culture. The historic city center is a 10-minute walk from the main station.

Nuremberg (Alternative Route)

Famous for its medieval castle, Nazi documentation center, and traditional gingerbread. The old town is directly adjacent to the main station.

Thuringian Forest

The direct route passes through Germany's legendary forest region. Window seats on the right side offer the best views of rolling hills and traditional villages.

Station Information

Berlin Hauptbahnhof

  • Location: Central Berlin, Mitte district
  • Transport: U-Bahn (S5, S7, S75), buses, trams
  • Facilities: Luggage storage, restaurants, shops, DB Lounge
  • Tip: Europe's largest crossing station - allow extra time for platform changes

München Hauptbahnhof

  • Location: Central Munich, walking distance to Marienplatz
  • Transport: U-Bahn (U1, U2, U4, U5, U7, U8), S-Bahn, trams
  • Facilities: Tourist info, luggage storage, car rentals, DB Lounge
  • Tip: Buy Munich day tickets at station machines before boarding

FAQ

Is there a direct train from Berlin to Munich?

Yes, Deutsche Bahn operates direct ICE trains every hour, with no changes required. The journey takes approximately 4 hours.

What's the cheapest way to travel from Berlin to Munich?

Book Sparpreis fares 2-3 months in advance (from €17.90). Combine with BahnCard 25 for additional 25% savings. Avoid peak travel times for best prices.

Can I use a Eurail Pass for this journey?

Yes, Eurail Global Pass and German Rail Pass cover this route. However, seat reservations are highly recommended (€4.50) especially during peak times. For a single trip, Sparpreis tickets are usually cheaper than a pass.

What's the difference between ICE and ICE Sprinter?

ICE Sprinter makes fewer stops (only major cities), completing the journey in 3h 52m vs 4h 15m for regular ICE. Sprinter tickets cost slightly more but save 20-30 minutes.

Is the train better than flying?

For Berlin-Munich, the train is highly competitive. City-center to city-center takes 4 hours vs 4.5+ hours flying (including airport transfers). The train offers more comfort, no security queues, and scenic views. Flying only makes sense for connecting flights.
