1. Glacier Express

The Glacier Express is known as the "world's slowest express train." Covering approximately 290 km across 291 bridges and 91 tunnels, it is Switzerland's most famous scenic train. Streckeninformation Route Zermatt → St. Moritz (or reverse) Distance Approx. 290 km Dauer Approx. 8 hours Operator Matterhorn Gotthard Bahn / Rhaetian Railway Fare CHF 152 (second class), reservation fee CHF 44 Highlights 291 bridges, 91 tunnels, Landwasser Viaduct

2. Bernina Express

The Bernina Express is a UNESCO World Heritage route, traveling from Chur or St. Moritz in Switzerland across the Alps to Tirano in Italy. Streckeninformation Route Chur/St. Moritz → Tirano (Italy) Distance Approx. 170 km (St. Moritz segment) Dauer Approx. 4 hours Operator Rhaetian Railway Fare CHF 63 (second class), reservation fee CHF 14 Highlights 196 bridges, 55 tunnels, Brusio Spiral Bridge
Feature Details
Beste Sitze Seat Selection Tips St. Moritz → Tirano: Choose right side
Tirano → St. Moritz: Choose left side
Panoramic car: First class has larger windows
Open-air car: Open in summer, best for photography

3. GoldenPass Line

Die GoldenPass-Linie verbindet Luzern und Montreux über Interlaken, eine der klassischsten Panoramastrecken der Schweiz. Streckeninformation Route Lucerne → Interlaken → Montreux Distance Approx. 200 km Dauer Approx. 5 hours Operator Swiss Federal Railways / BLS / MOB Fare CHF 70 (second class), Swiss Travel Pass included Highlights Lake Brienz, Jungfrau, vineyard terraces

7. Buchungstipps

Booking Checklist
    Book 3 months ahead (especially for summer) Choose panoramic car (first class) Reserve window seat (right or left depending on direction) Consider Swiss Travel Pass (better value) Book meals (available on some trains) Bring a jacket (air conditioning is cold) Prepare your camera (scenery is stunning)
Important Tips Swiss scenic trains are extremely popular — book 3 months ahead for summer (July–August). Some alpine routes may close in winter due to weather; confirm before traveling.
